Tablet XII: Appendix

Why Tablet XII Is Different

Andrew George explains that Tablet XII is not part of The Epic of Gilgamesh itself. It is an Akkadian translation of the latter part of the older Sumerian poem Bilgames and the Netherworld.

It was probably attached to the “Series of Gilgamesh” because its description of the Netherworld was relevant to Gilgamesh’s later association with the dead.

In this edition, the text is not presented here as a normal twelfth tablet. Its translation appears later in Chapter 5, after the translation of the original Sumerian poem.

How to Read It

Tablet XI should be treated as the true conclusion of Gilgamesh’s journey. Tablet XII is supplementary material rather than the next stage of the plot.

Readers interested in the appendix can continue to Chapter 5, where the book presents both the original Sumerian poem and the later Akkadian version.

Why It Was Appended

The appendix adds information about Mesopotamian beliefs concerning the Netherworld and the condition of the dead.

Its relevance is thematic rather than narrative: it connects with the epic’s larger interest in death, burial, memory, and Gilgamesh’s relationship to the world of the dead.
